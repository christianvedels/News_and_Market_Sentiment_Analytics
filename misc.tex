\subsection{Variance} 
By definition the variance of a weighted mean is given by 
\begin{equation}
    \hat{y}_k = \sum_{i=1}^n w_{ik} y_i,\quad \sum_{i=1}^n w_{ik} = 1
\end{equation}
Assuming the $y_i$ are uncorrelated with variances $\sigma_i^2 = \mathrm{Var}(y_i)$, the variance of $\hat{y}_k$ is
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{Var}(\hat{y}_k) = \sum_{i=1}^n w_{ik}^2 \,\sigma_i^2.
\end{equation}
More generally, if $\Sigma$ is the covariance matrix of $y=(y_1,\dots,y_n)^\top$ and $w_k=(w_{1k},\dots,w_{nk})^\top$, then
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{Var}(\hat{y}_k) = w_k^\top \Sigma\, w_k.
\end{equation}
